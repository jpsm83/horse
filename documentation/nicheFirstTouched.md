## Section 5 - Strategy: Start Narrow, Become The Platform

This is exactly where founders make a mistake.

You’re already drifting toward:

“one app for everything in horses”

That sounds attractive, but it’s usually how startups die.

Because if you don’t know the niche yet, building “everything” means building the wrong everything.

Instead:

Find the highest pain, highest willingness-to-pay workflow, dominate that, then expand into the ecosystem.

Think of it like this:

Step 1: become indispensable
Step 2: become the platform

Not the reverse.

First: understand the horse market

This industry is actually many industries pretending to be one.

Different users have different problems:

1. Horse owners (wealthy individuals)

## Section 6 - Strategy Refinement: SaaS First, Marketplace Second

I think that idea is better than it sounds, but only if you structure it correctly.

What you’re describing is basically:

“Booking + marketplace + reputation layer for the horse industry”

That can work.

But there’s a trap:

Pure marketplaces are brutally hard.

Why?

Chicken-and-egg problem:

customers don’t come without providers
providers don’t join without customers

Especially in horse niches where trust matters.

So the question becomes:

How do you get supply first?

And this is where your previous idea becomes powerful.

The strongest version of your business

Don’t build:

“Horse Yelp”

That becomes low-value.

Instead build:

Stable OS + trusted marketplace

Meaning:

Phase 1 → B2B SaaS

Sell software to stables/trainers.

This gets you:

horse data
stable relationships
trust
supply side
recurring revenue

Features:

stable management
owner communication
horse profiles
invoices
medical docs
scheduling

You make money immediately.

Example:
€99–399/month per stable.

Phase 2 → Service marketplace

Now providers are already inside.

Enable discovery.

Trainers

## Section 7 - Product Shape: Stable Management Cloud

I would lean even harder toward B2B SaaS first, marketplace second.

The mistake many founders make is building a marketplace because it sounds exciting:

profiles
reviews
social features
buying/selling
messaging

But marketplaces are mostly a distribution problem, not a software problem.

A SaaS has a much simpler question:

"Can I save this business enough time, money, or headaches that they'll pay me every month?"

That's a much easier business to build.

Stable Management Cloud

Think:

Shopify for horse businesses.

Not just stables.
